# ontario-public-sector-workforce-analysis

**Ontario Sunshine List - Public Sector Workforce Analysis**

Ontario Sunshine List published on a calendar year basis, contains the list of all public sector employees earning more than 100K CAD. This list is published as per the public sector salary disclosure act to provide greater transparency and accountability In this Business Intelligence project using POWER BI as a business analysis tool, the Ontario Sunshine list containing the salary, names, positions and entity of individuals for the last three calendar years 2023- 2025 pertaining to ~0.5 million data points were cleaned, grouped and analyzed to extract key findings.

**Business Problem**
The current option while allowing viewers to see/search by individual names or entity does not provide interactive options to analyse this rich information. The dashboard transforms publicly available Sunshine List data into actionable insights by analyzing compensation trends, payroll growth, and workforce composition across public sector organizations from 2023–2025. The solution enables users to benchmark sectors, identify emerging compensation patterns, and explore payroll trends beyond individual employee searches.

**Business Analysis & Data Analytics Tools**

1.Data Extraction and Preparation 2.Power Query (ETL & Data Transformation) 3.Data Modeling (Star Schema Design) 4.DAX Measures and Calculations 5.Power BI Visualization & Dashboard Development 6.Data Analysis and Insight Generation

**Key Findings**

1.Ontario Power Generation (OPG) dominated the Sunshine List's top earnings rankings from 2023–2025. Its highest-paid executive earned $2.01M in 2024 and $1.90M in 2025, with several other OPG executives also appearing among the province's top earners. The average Sunshine List salary reached approximately $134K in 2025, representing a modest 1.3% increase over 2024.With inflation at 2.1%, real purchasing power declined for Sunshine List employees.

2.Total Sunshine List payroll exceeded $54B in 2025, equivalent to approximately 4.4% of Ontario's GDP.

3.School Boards employed the largest number of Sunshine List employees but reported the lowest median salary among all sectors.

4.OPG consistently recorded the highest median salary despite having a significantly smaller workforce, making it a notable outlier in public sector compensation.

5.A similar pattern was observed in taxable benefits: School Boards reported the lowest median taxable benefits, while OPG maintained the highest levels across all years analyzed. Universities ranked second in median salary, highlighting a substantial compensation gap between post-secondary and K–12 education sectors.
